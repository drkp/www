I am a PhD student at the University of Washington working with Hank Levy and Arvind Krishnamurthy in the Systems and Networking group. My research interest is primarily in computer systems, especially operating systems and distributed systems.


Before starting my PhD, I worked for three years at VMware in the virtual machine monitor group on VM checkpointing, memory management and page sharing.

I received my B.S. in computer science from MIT in 2008 and my M. Eng. in 2009. At MIT, I worked with Jeremy Stribling, Robert Morris and Frans Kaashoek in the Parallel and Distributed Operating Systems group.

I was born in Beijing, China and spent most of my time growing up in Columbus, Indiana. My husband and I like to cook, travel and occasionally do computer science together.
