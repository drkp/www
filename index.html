I am a PhD student at the University of Washington working with Hank Levy and Arvind Krishnamurthy in the Systems and Networking group. My research interest is primarily in computer systems, especially operating systems and distributed systems.

Present

Currently, my work focuses on two areas. First, I am designing new operating system abstractions for mobile/cloud applications. The applications that users interact with daily no longer run on a single desktop computer, but instead span mobile devices and cloud servers. Modern application programmers face significant development challenges without the OS support that traditional desktop applications benefit from. My work re-designs traditional OS services for modern mobile/cloud applications.

Second, I am working on improving protocols for distributed transactional storage systems by co-designing distributed protocols that are traditionally implemented separately. Integrating these protocols allows us to take advantage of cross-layer optimizations, significantly improving performance and simplifying the protocols.

Past

Before starting my PhD, I worked for three years at VMware in the virtual machine monitor group on VM checkpointing, memory management and page sharing.

I received my B.S. in computer science from MIT in 2008 and my M. Eng. in 2009. At MIT, I worked with Jeremy Stribling, Robert Morris and Frans Kaashoek in the Parallel and Distributed Operating Systems group.
