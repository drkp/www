About Me

Present

I am a PhD student at the University of Washington working with Hank Levy, Arvind Krishnamurthy and Steve Gribble in the Systems and Networking group. My research interests are generally in operating systems and distributed systems. I am currently focusing on systems that support large-scale distributed applications.

Past

Before starting my PhD, I worked for three years at VMware in the virtual machine monitor group on VM checkpointing, memory management and page sharing.

I received my B.S. in computer science from MIT in 2008 and my M. Eng. in 2009. At MIT, I worked with Jeremy Stribling, Robert Morris and Frans Kaashoek in the Parallel and Distributed Operating Systems group.

Personal

I was born in Beijing, China and spent most of my time growing up in Columbus, Indiana. My husband and I like to cook, travel and occasionally do computer science.
